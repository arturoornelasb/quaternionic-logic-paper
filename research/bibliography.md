# Bibliography — Literature discovered during OQ investigation

References organized by topic. Verified from training data (May 2025 cutoff).
DOIs should be confirmed before citing in paper.

---

## Non-commutative residuated lattices (OQ1)

- Galatos, N., Jipsen, P., Kowalski, T. & Ono, H. (2007). *Residuated
  Lattices: An Algebraic Glimpse at Substructural Logics*. Elsevier.
  [Standard reference for non-commutative residuated lattices]

- Hajek, P. (2003). "Observations on non-commutative fuzzy logic."
  *Soft Computing* 7(1). [psBL-algebras with two residuals]

- Ciungu, L.C. (2006+). Non-commutative extensions of BL-algebras.
  Various papers in *Soft Computing* and *Studia Logica*.

- Dai, S. (2023). "Quaternionic fuzzy sets." *Axioms* 12(5), 490.
  [Set operations only, no logic]

## Log-prime embeddings (OQ3)

- Birkhoff, G. (1967). *Lattice Theory*, 3rd ed. AMS.
  [Divisibility lattice coordinatization via exponent vectors]

- Hardy, G.H. & Wright, E.M. (1938+). *An Introduction to the Theory
  of Numbers*. Oxford. [Prime factorization as vector — implicit]

- Scheinerman, E.R. & Ullman, D.H. (1997). *Fractional Graph Theory*.
  Wiley. [Log-prime embedding of divisibility posets into R^k]

## Many-valued modal logic (OQ5 — modal)

- Fitting, M. (1991). "Many-valued modal logics." *Fundamenta
  Informaticae* 15(3-4), 235-254. [Kripke over lattices; {0,1} = classical]

- Fitting, M. (1992). "Many-valued modal logics II." *Fundamenta
  Informaticae* 17, 55-73. [Extension to infinite lattices]

- Caicedo, X. & Rodriguez, R. (2010). "Standard Godel modal logics."
  *Studia Logica* 94(2), 189-214. [Completeness on [0,1]; S4/S5 exact
  at {0,1}. KEY REFERENCE for upgrading P11 modal recovery.]

- Bou, F., Esteva, F., Godo, L. & Rodriguez, R. (2011). "On the
  minimum many-valued modal logic over a finite residuated lattice."
  *J. Logic and Computation* 21(5), 739-790.

## Trivalent logic recovery (OQ5 — trivalent)

- Cignoli, R., D'Ottaviano, I. & Mundici, D. (2000). *Algebraic
  Foundations of Many-Valued Reasoning*. Kluwer. [L3 = exact subalgebra
  of [0,1] MV-algebra at {0,1/2,1}. KEY REFERENCE for P11.]

## Fibring of logics (OQ5 — general)

- Gabbay, D. (1999). *Fibring Logics*. Oxford UP. [Component logics
  exactly recoverable by projection from fibred logic]

- Sernadas, C., Sernadas, A. & Caleiro, C. (1999). "Fibring of logics
  as a categorial construction." *J. Logic and Computation* 9(2), 149-179.

## Belnap-Dunn four-valued logic (OQ5 — uncharted)

- Belnap, N. (1977). "A useful four-valued logic." In *Modern Uses of
  Multiple-Valued Logic*, Reidel. [FOUR bilattice; no quaternion connection]

- Priest, G. (2008). "Many-valued modal logics: a simple approach."
  *Review of Symbolic Logic* 1(2), 190-203. [Modal logic over De Morgan
  lattices; FOUR gives paraconsistent modal logic]

## Clifford algebras and two actions of H (OQ6)

- Porteous, I.R. (1995). *Clifford Algebras and the Classical Groups*.
  Cambridge. Ch. 10. [Left action + conjugation in Cl(0,2)]

- Hestenes, D. & Sobczyk, G. (1984). *Clifford Algebra to Geometric
  Calculus*. Reidel. [Left mult vs sandwich product distinguished]

- Doran, C. & Lasenby, A. (2003). *Geometric Algebra for Physicists*.
  Cambridge. [Same distinction, physics perspective]

- Lounesto, P. (2001). *Clifford Algebras and Spinors*, 2nd ed.
  Cambridge. [Spinors = left ideal (left action), vectors = sandwich]

- Lam, T.Y. (1999). *Lectures on Modules and Rings*. Springer.
  [H as (H,H)-bimodule; Morita theory unifies both actions]

- Stillwell, J. (2008). *Naive Lie Theory*. Springer.
  [Kernel {+/-1} of SU(2)->SO(3) explains iteration difference]

## Hegelian synthesis formalized (OQ2)

- Lawvere, F.W. (1969). "Adjointness in Foundations." *Dialectica* 23.
  [Adjoint pairs as fundamental logic; monad = synthesis. SEMINAL.]

- Lawvere, F.W. (1996). "Unity and Identity of Opposites in Calculus
  and Physics." *Applied Categorical Structures* 4. [Hegel formalized
  via adjunctions]

- Reyes, M.C. & Zolfaghari, H. (2004). "Bi-Heyting Algebras, Toposes
  and Modalities." *J. Philosophical Logic* 33. [Co-Heyting + Heyting
  = thesis/antithesis coexistence]

- nLab entry "Aufhebung" (Schreiber, Shulman, et al.). [Aufhebung as
  smallest higher topos level sublating a given level. Sharpest modern
  formalization.]
